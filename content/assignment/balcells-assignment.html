Instructions

Answer the questions (below), and keep track of your answers somewhere (a notepad?). You will input your answers into Canvas. Figure out the answers BEFORE opening Canvas, otherwise you’ll run out of time.

Skills:

  • counting categories
  • creating variables
  • sorting

Data

You’re going to work with data from the Kalyvas and Balcells article we read last week. The dataset contains information on major civil wars around the world.

  • Download this: Kalyvas and Balcells
Variable Description
countryname Country where civil war takes place
year/month_start Year/month war began
year/month_end Year/month war ended (if at all)
conflict_name Name of conflict
region World region
gdp_capita GDP per capita (logged)
rough_terrain Percent of country covered in rough terrain
military_personnel_percapita Military personnel, in thousands
marxist Is rebellion Marxist?
technologyrebellion What kind of civil war is it?
ethnicwar Is this an ethnic civil war?

Questions

Using the dataset, answer the following questions:

  1. Choose a civil war of each technology type identified by the authors and look it up on Wikipedia. Provide a non-technical, 3 sentence description of each war. (total = 3 wars, 3 sentences each).

  2. Calculate how long each civil war has lasted (year ended - year started. Note: not all civil wars have ended / have an end date). What is the longest lasting civil war, for which an end date is available?

  3. What is the shortest civil war in Latin America?

  4. What percent of civil wars are ethnic civil wars?

  5. Which region has the fewest number of listed civil wars?

  6. About what percent of civil wars are Marxist?

  7. Which country had the largest army involved in a civil war?

  8. Which country in Eurasia had the smallest army fight a civil war?
